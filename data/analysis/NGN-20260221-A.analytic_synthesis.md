# **Analysis Note: NGN-20260221-A — 小諸本陣主屋**

---

**record\_id:** NGN-20260221-A

**analytical\_identity:** Dataset's first nighttime indoor record, first high-scoring artificial\_warm lighting record, first heritage adaptive reuse space, and first enclosure\_deep geometry type. Paired with NGN-20260222-A (Mariko Winery) as consecutive observations from the same Nagano trip, forming the dataset's first indoor/outdoor, enclosed/open, artificial/natural light contrast pair. Also the second record documenting mid-visit information retrieval altering the emotional trajectory (after KYT-20260118-A Katsura), establishing this as a recurring observer behavior pattern rather than an isolated event.

**dimensional\_signature:** Vt=8.5 / Vl=8.5 / Ve=8.5 / Vr=8 / Vg=6.5. Triple-8.5 cluster across temporal density, light quality, and emotional resonance, with rhythm close behind. Vg is the clear outlier — 2 points below the top scores. This is the dataset's first record where geometry is explicitly acknowledged as the weakest dimension by the observer herself ("真要說幾何結構，這裡似乎沒有過多著墨"), and the score reflects that honesty. The profile shape — four strong dimensions held together despite a geometric deficit — suggests that in heritage dining spaces, atmospheric coherence (light \+ rhythm \+ cultural depth \+ emotional time) can compensate for geometric simplicity. This is structurally the inverse of Katsura (Vg=9 with Ve=5.5): Katsura had perfect geometry but suppressed emotion; Komoro has modest geometry but sustained emotional resonance.

**primary\_tension:** Heritage weight vs. present-tense experience. The exit\_state names this directly: "遺產的厚重感不斷提醒這裡曾有名人歇腳，而fine dining的精緻進程又不斷拉回現在的味覺高潮，形成雙向時間流." The space pulls the observer simultaneously toward Edo-period history (Vt) and toward the immediate sensory present (Ve via taste, light, warmth). This is not a tension that depresses scores — it is a productive oscillation that sustains engagement across a three-hour meal. The key\_insight captures the mechanism: cultural heritage is not backdrop but active participant in the dining rhythm.

**emotional\_profile:** Ascending resonance across an extended duration. Entry is atmospheric — the light transition from bright entrance to dim interior creates immediate temporal deceleration. The turning point is mid-meal active information retrieval: the observer queries the restaurant's ownership during dinner, discovers the Fujiya Gohonjin connection, and reframes the space from "renovated old building" to "high-capital cultural preservation operation." This reframe deepens rather than redirects the emotional arc — it adds an intellectual layer (respect for cultural capital deployment) to an already positive bodily experience (warmth, slow pace, good food). Exit is sustained comfort with desire for return. The three-hour duration without perceived time passage is strong Ve evidence. Note: Ve=8.5 is supported by both intrinsic spatial response and preloaded knowledge activation, though the trigger is labeled `external_narrative_AI` based on the mid-meal search. The trigger classification reflects the moment of reframe, not the dominant emotional source.

**methodological\_relevance:** High on three counts. First, this record proves that SDIndex's Vl dimension can differentiate artificial light quality — Komoro's artificial\_warm Vl=8.5 vs. LaLaport's artificial\_neutral Vl=2.0 is now the dataset's cleanest same-source-type contrast. Second, it demonstrates that high SDIndex (8.0) is achievable with a geometric deficit (Vg=6.5), establishing that dimensional convergence is not required for high total scores — atmospheric coherence across non-geometric dimensions can compensate. Third, it is the second record documenting mid-visit information retrieval altering emotion trajectory, which at N=6 is enough to flag this as a systematic observer behavior warranting future tracking.

**research\_value:** The Komoro-Mariko pair (NGN-20260221-A ↔ NGN-20260222-A) is the dataset's first structurally designed contrast pair from the same trip. They share: same region, same observer, same companion, same season, consecutive days. They differ on: time of day (night vs. morning), light source (artificial\_warm vs. natural\_direct), geometry (enclosure\_deep vs. open\_unframed), spatial character (indoor heritage reuse vs. outdoor agricultural landscape). Both score SDIndex 8.0-8.7. This pair can answer: do fundamentally different spatial typologies produce the same SDIndex range through different dimensional pathways? Early evidence says yes — Mariko leads with Vl/Vr/Ve convergence in open space; Komoro leads with Vt/Vl/Ve convergence in enclosed space. Same altitude, different routes.
