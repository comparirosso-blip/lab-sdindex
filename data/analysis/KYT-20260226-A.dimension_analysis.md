## **Vt — Temporal/Cultural Density**

**score:** 8 **subtype\_or\_source:** architectural\_heritage **analytical\_role\_in\_this\_record:** Vt is the vertical axis of this record — literally and conceptually. The field note establishes density through enumeration (寺院、町家、歷史街區), and the turning point extends this into a claim about historical persistence under contemporary pressure. Vt supplies the "what is here that has always been here" layer against which every other dimension operates. **how\_it\_interacts\_with\_other\_dimensions:** Vt provides the fixed reference that Vg anchors to (the pagoda as geometric anchor depends on the pagoda being a heritage object, not just a tall structure). It also sets the tension baseline for Vr — crowd density becomes meaningful precisely because what the crowd moves through is historically dense. The key\_insight phrase「歷史垂直軸 vs 現代水平密度」explicitly frames Vt as one pole of the record's central dialectic. **whether\_it\_is\_primary\_secondary\_or\_supporting:** Primary.

## **Vl — Light Quality**

**score:** 6 **subtype\_or\_source:** natural\_diffused **analytical\_role\_in\_this\_record:** Vl operates as the atmospheric envelope. The overcast sky produces flat, even illumination — the field note acknowledges this flatness but notes that angular variation against surfaces still produces some drama. The entry\_state reinforces this: 「低飽和的灰藍色，光線柔而平，街道與屋瓦的輪廓反而更加清晰。」 The key function of Vl here is not spectacle but legibility — diffused light strips away shadow distraction and makes architectural contour and material texture more readable. **how\_it\_interacts\_with\_other\_dimensions:** Vl serves Vt and Vg by increasing structural clarity. Without directional light creating dramatic highlights, the eye reads form and surface rather than chasing light effects. This is why the observer can perceive geometric anchor shifts on the stairway (Vg) and register historical material density (Vt) even in a crowded, noisy environment. Vl quietly improves the signal-to-noise ratio for the two high-scoring dimensions. **whether\_it\_is\_primary\_secondary\_or\_supporting:** Supporting.

## **Vr — Social/Rhythm Density**

**score:** 7 **subtype\_or\_source:** invited\_openness (as labeled; noted in QC as potentially misaligned) **analytical\_role\_in\_this\_record:** Vr carries the horizontal axis of the record's central tension. The field note records crowd density as the primary observation — people are present in volume, even in off-season. But the analytical weight of Vr in this record is not about the crowd itself; it is about what the crowd fails to destroy. The turning point's core observation is that heritage structures「在人潮與現代聲響之間維持著一種奇特的平衡」. Vr=7 registers not crowd pleasure but crowd pressure as a test condition — and the space passes. **how\_it\_interacts\_with\_other\_dimensions:** Vr is Vt's antagonist and Vt's proof. High crowd density should degrade heritage atmosphere, but the record argues it does not. This produces the record's central finding: spatial structure persists under crowd load. Vr also interacts with Ve negatively — the crowd does not generate emotional resonance (Ve=2.5), only ambient tolerance. The gap between Vr=7 and Ve=2.5 is itself informative: high social density, low emotional trigger. **whether\_it\_is\_primary\_secondary\_or\_supporting:** Secondary. It is essential to the record's argument but functions as a condition variable, not as the dimension that carries aesthetic value.

## **Vg — Geometric/Spatial Composition**

**score:** 8 **subtype\_or\_source:** open\_unframed (as labeled; QC flagged misalignment with field note) **analytical\_role\_in\_this\_record:** Vg is the record's structural engine. The field note describes a specific mechanism: the stairway's elevation changes shift the viewer's angle on the pagoda, creating a moving geometric anchor. This is not static composition — it is kinetic framing produced by the observer's own movement through sloped terrain. Vg=8 is high because the geometry is not merely present but dynamically responsive to pedestrian motion. **how\_it\_interacts\_with\_other\_dimensions:** Vg and Vt are tightly coupled in this record. The pagoda is both a heritage object (Vt) and a geometric anchor (Vg), and the stairway is both a historical street form (Vt) and the mechanism that generates angular variation (Vg). This is the record's strongest dimension pair — they share physical referents but measure different properties of those referents. Vg also benefits from Vl's diffused flatness, which makes the pagoda silhouette and roofline geometry legible against the grey sky without competing shadow drama. **whether\_it\_is\_primary\_secondary\_or\_supporting:** Primary (co-primary with Vt).

## **Ve — Emotional Resonance**

**score:** 2.5 **subtype\_or\_source:** external\_narrative\_human (as labeled; QC flagged weak provenance) **analytical\_role\_in\_this\_record:** Ve is deliberately low and this is analytically significant. The observer registers no strong emotional trigger — no awe, no nostalgia, no discomfort severe enough to provoke flight. The Ve note describes a condition of emotional neutrality with mild positive inflection: crowd mood is tolerable, kimono-wearing tourists produce a faint temporal-overlay sensation. Ve=2.5 says: this space does not need to move you emotionally to function aesthetically. The exit\_state's claim (「去掉寧靜之後，場所的本質反而更顯現」) depends on Ve being low — if Ve were high, the record would be about feeling, not about structure. **how\_it\_interacts\_with\_other\_dimensions:** Ve's low value is what makes the Vt-Vg dominance interpretable. If Ve were 7 or 8, the record would read as an emotional experience report. At 2.5, it reads as a structural observation made under emotionally neutral conditions. Ve also provides negative evidence for sensory\_resilience: the space absorbs crowd energy without producing observer distress (Ve does not spike negative) but also without producing observer elation (Ve does not spike positive). The space is resilient precisely because it operates below the emotional threshold. **whether\_it\_is\_primary\_secondary\_or\_supporting:** Supporting — but its low value is analytically load-bearing. It is a quiet dimension that does significant work by staying quiet.

## **Synthesis**

**dominant\_dimension\_pattern:** Vt–Vg co-dominance. Both score 8\. They share physical referents (pagoda, stairway, heritage streetscape) but measure distinct properties — temporal density and geometric composition. The record's analytical center is the interaction between these two.

**dimensional\_balance\_or\_imbalance:** Structurally top-heavy. Vt=8 and Vg=8 carry the record. Vr=7 serves as the stress-test condition. Vl=6 provides atmospheric support. Ve=2.5 is intentionally low. This is not a balanced sensory profile — it is a record designed to test whether structural dimensions (Vt, Vg) hold up when atmospheric and emotional dimensions (Vl, Ve) are muted and social pressure (Vr) is high. The imbalance is the argument.

**core\_interaction\_map:**

Vt ←→ Vg: co-anchored through shared physical referents (pagoda, stairway). Strongest coupling in the record.

Vt ←→ Vr: dialectical tension. Heritage persistence tested against crowd flow. The key\_insight「歷史垂直軸 vs 現代水平密度」names this axis directly.

Vl → Vt, Vg: enabling relationship. Diffused light increases legibility of structure and form, amplifying the two dominant dimensions without competing for attention.

Vr ↔ Ve: decoupled. High crowd density does not produce high emotional response. This gap is evidence for the record's implicit claim about spatial resilience.

Ve → record framing: Ve's low value shifts the record from experiential report to structural analysis. It is a framing condition, not a content dimension.

**summary\_of\_how\_the\_record\_holds\_together:** This record makes a specific, testable claim: Ninenzaka's aesthetic structure is architecturally and geometrically self-sustaining — it does not require seasonal decoration (cherry blossoms, autumn leaves), favorable light, crowd absence, or emotional intensity to register as a high-density sensory environment. The dimensional profile supports this claim coherently. Vt and Vg are high because the heritage architecture and kinetic stairway geometry are inherent to the site. Vl is moderate because overcast light is adequate, not spectacular. Vr is high because the crowd is present and the space absorbs it. Ve is low because the record is not about feeling — it is about whether the structure stands without emotional support.

The record's exit\_state (「去掉櫻花之後，結構更清楚。去掉寧靜之後，場所的本質反而更顯現。」) is the verbal compression of this dimensional logic: subtract the seasonal variable, subtract the quietness variable, and the Vt-Vg scaffold remains. Whether this claim is fully supported by a single record is a QC question (addressed in EB-02 of the prior review). But as a dimensional reading, the internal logic is consistent — the scores, notes, and trajectory all point in the same direction.
